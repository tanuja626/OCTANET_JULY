Tropical Rainforests

Found near the equator, these forests are characterized by high rainfall, warm temperatures year-round, and incredible biodiversity. They have dense canopies that allow little light to reach the forest floor, resulting in a rich undergrowth of shade-tolerant plants.

Temperate Deciduous Forests

Located in regions with distinct seasons, including cold winters and warm summers. These forests are dominated by deciduous trees such as oak, maple, and beech, which shed their leaves annually. They support a diverse range of wildlife and have a well-developed understory.

Boreal Forests (Taiga)

Found in the high northern latitudes, these forests are characterized by long, cold winters and short, mild summers. They consist mainly of coniferous trees like spruce, fir, and pine, adapted to withstand harsh conditions. The boreal forest is crucial for carbon storage and houses numerous animal species adapted to the cold.

Tropical Dry Forests

These forests occur in regions with seasonal rainfall, leading to distinct wet and dry seasons. Trees in these forests, such as teak and mahogany, often shed their leaves during the dry season to conserve water. They are less dense than rainforests but still support diverse wildlife and vegetation.

Temperate Rainforests

Found in coastal regions with high rainfall and moderate temperatures, these forests are characterized by evergreen trees such as Douglas fir and redwood. They have lush undergrowth, including ferns and mosses, and play a vital role in carbon sequestration.

Mediterranean Forests

Located in regions with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters, these forests are adapted to withstand drought and fire. They typically feature a mix of evergreen and deciduous trees, shrubs, and herbaceous plants. The Mediterranean forests are biodiversity hotspots, home to many endemic species.

Mangrove Forests

These unique coastal forests grow in tidal zones of tropical and subtropical regions. Mangroves are specially adapted to thrive in salty, oxygen-poor soil and protect coastlines from erosion and storms. They provide crucial habitats for fish, birds, and other wildlife.

Montane Forests

Found in mountainous regions, these forests vary with altitude and climate. Lower montane forests are often similar to tropical or temperate forests, while higher elevations are characterized by coniferous trees and alpine vegetation. These forests are important for water regulation and biodiversity conservation.
